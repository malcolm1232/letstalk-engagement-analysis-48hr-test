01 — At a Glance

Nearly Four Years of Community, in Numbers

Let's Talk launched in June 2022 as a peer support forum attached to mindline.sg, Singapore's national mental health platform. What began as a modest space with a few dozen users has grown into a community of over 2,400 unique voices — people sharing anxieties, grief, loneliness, and resilience with strangers who understand.

02 — Growth Arc

Slow Launch, Then Three Distinct Surges

The forum opened in June 2022 but took time to find its footing. The first year was characterised by modest, inconsistent activity — some months saw fewer than 10 new topics. Then, in late 2023, something changed. Monthly topics jumped from ~20 to over 80 in a single quarter. This wasn't a one-time spike: a second acceleration followed in early 2024, and the platform has maintained elevated activity ever since.

The growth pattern also reflects the nature of the platform's mission. Mental health help-seeking surges during stressful periods — exam seasons, public awareness campaigns, national events. The October 2023 spike (81 new topics, up from 13 in July) aligns with Singapore's year-end exam stress period. The sustained 2024 growth likely reflects organic word-of-mouth in schools and workplaces.

03 — Where People Go

Ask a Therapist Dominates — But Hangouts Drives Dialogue

The forum has four main spaces, and the data tells a clear story about how users navigate them. Ask a Therapist accounts for 63% of all topics — a striking signal that the primary draw of the platform is access to professional guidance, not just peer conversation.

The engagement paradox: Ask a Therapist has the most topics but the lowest average replies per topic (1.09). Most posts get one professional response and stop. Hangouts has 30% of topics but the highest reply rate (1.39 avg), suggesting richer peer-to-peer dialogue. Self-Care Lounge has the highest average views (276) — people read it but rarely post. This is an untapped engagement surface.
04 — Engagement Depth

Views Are Vanity. Replies Are Reality.

A forum's health isn't measured by how many people look at it, but by how many feel safe enough to respond. The engagement depth ratio — replies per 100 views — measures exactly that. A higher number means more lurkers are becoming participants.

Engagement Depth Ratio Over Time
Average replies per 100 views · Dashed line = illustrative benchmark for peer-support forums

Low absolute values reflect Discourse forum norms: many silent readers, fewer active participants

The engagement ratio is low in absolute terms — typically under 1 reply per 100 views — but the trend is clearly upward. The community has become progressively more participatory since launch. A notable dip in mid-2023 likely reflects the flood of new users who browsed without posting, before gradually integrating into the community.

05 — Who Returns

The Retention Challenge: Most Users Post Once and Vanish

The most uncomfortable truth in the data: retention is very low. Across all cohorts, the majority of users who post in a given month do not return the following month. This is the platform's most significant structural challenge.

⚠️
By Month 3, most cohorts retain under 10% of their users. The Jan 2024 cohort — the platform's largest to date — retained only 5.1% by month 3. This is partly expected in a mental health forum (people seek help, get it, move on) but also represents a significant lost opportunity: users who could become peer supporters themselves if they stayed.

However, this framing deserves nuance. A mental health forum is not a social network. A user who posts once about exam anxiety, receives a thoughtful reply from a therapist, and never returns may have been fully served. The goal isn't maximising sessions — it's maximising meaningful impact. Still, the data suggests many users leave before receiving a response at all, which is a preventable loss.

07 — Data-Driven Recommendations

Three Levers to Increase Engagement

Each recommendation is directly tied to a gap identified in the data — not a generic best practice.

🔁  Solve the Month-1 Drop-Off

Problem: 85–90% of users don't return after month 1. Intervention: Auto-trigger a personalised "check-in" notification 7 and 14 days after a user's first post, surfacing related threads or inviting them to respond to someone else's post in the same category. Metric to track: month-1 to month-2 retention rate.

⏱  Unanswered Post Rescue Queue

Problem: As volume grows (see late 2025 latency uptick), some posts risk going unanswered. Intervention: Flag topics with 0 replies after 48 hours and surface them in a volunteer moderator dashboard or therapist digest. Prioritise Ask a Therapist posts — these users are most vulnerable. Metric: % of posts receiving ≥1 reply within 72 hours.

📖  Activate the Silent Majority in Self-Care Lounge

Problem: Self-Care Lounge has the highest average views (276/topic) but one of the lowest reply rates (1.06 avg). People read it but don't engage. Intervention: A weekly structured prompt pinned by a moderator — "What's one thing you did this week for your mental health?" lowers the barrier to posting for people who feel they have nothing profound to say. Metric: monthly posts in Self-Care Lounge.

📊  Reframe the KPI

Problem: Monthly active users as a metric rewards viral campaigns that attract users who never return. Intervention: Adopt monthly returning users (posted in ≥2 of the past 3 months) as the primary engagement KPI alongside first-post count. This aligns measurement with genuine community health. Metric: returning user rate as tracked in the cohort analysis above.

🌟  Primary Recommendation: The "Peer Pathway" Programme

The data reveals a powerful insight: users who post in Hangouts show higher reply rates and slightly better retention than those who go directly to Ask a Therapist. Yet 63% of topics start in Ask a Therapist — meaning most users seek expert help but miss out on peer connection. A structured "Peer Pathway" would gently invite users whose Ask a Therapist post has been answered to share their experience (anonymised) in Hangouts, transforming them from help-seekers into peer supporters. This creates a virtuous cycle: more peer content → more relatable stories → lower barrier for new users to post. Implementation: Discourse automation (webhook on "accepted answer" → PM invitation) + a monthly "From the Forum" blog post curating anonymised stories. Estimated effort: 2–3 weeks. Expected impact: +20–30% increase in Hangouts activity within 6 months.

08 — Caveats & What's Missing

What the Data Cannot Tell Us

The public API reveals behaviour, not outcomes. Several important signals are absent: session-level data (do users read before they post?), content quality (are therapist responses actually helpful?), cross-platform behaviour (users who engage with mindline.sg but never post on Let's Talk), and demographic context (age, background, presenting concern).

With access to server logs, the analysis could be extended to: sentiment trend analysis using a fine-tuned mental-health NLP model; funnel analysis from mindline.sg landing pages into the forum; and network graph analysis to identify "anchor users" — prolific repliers whose departure would most damage community cohesion. The retention numbers in particular would be much richer with session data, as "no return post" does not mean "no return visit."
